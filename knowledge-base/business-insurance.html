💼 Business Insurance Knowledge Base

Protect your business from lawsuits, property damage, and employee injuries.

General Liability Insurance

The most fundamental business insurance. General liability protects your business from claims of:

  • Bodily injury: A customer slips in your store
  • Property damage: You damage a client's equipment
  • Advertising injury: Libel, slander, copyright infringement

Average cost: $400–$1,500/year depending on industry and size.

Professional Liability (E&O) Insurance

Protects against claims of negligence, errors, or failure to deliver professional services. Essential for:

  • Consultants and advisors
  • IT and tech companies
  • Real estate agents
  • Accountants and lawyers
  • Healthcare providers

Workers' Compensation

Required in almost every state if you have employees. Covers:

  • Medical expenses for work-related injuries
  • Lost wages during recovery
  • Disability benefits
  • Death benefits for families

Cost is typically $0.75–$2.74 per $100 of payroll, varying by industry and claims history.

Business Owner's Policy (BOP)

A bundled policy that combines general liability + commercial property insurance at a discount. Ideal for small to mid-size businesses. Typically costs 30–40% less than buying each policy separately.

Commercial Auto Insurance

If your business uses vehicles, personal auto insurance won't cover business use. Commercial auto covers company-owned or employee-driven vehicles used for business purposes.

Cyber Liability Insurance

Increasingly essential for any business that handles customer data. Covers:

  • Data breach notification costs
  • Credit monitoring for affected customers
  • Legal defense and settlements
  • Business interruption from cyber attacks
  • Ransomware payments (in some policies)
💡 Minimum Coverage for New Businesses

At minimum, most new businesses need: General Liability + Workers' Comp (if you have employees) + Professional Liability (if you provide services). A BOP bundles the first two at a discount.
