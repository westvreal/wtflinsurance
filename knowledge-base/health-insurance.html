Understanding Plan Types

HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)

Requires you to choose a primary care physician (PCP) and get referrals for specialists. You must use in-network providers.

  • Pros: Lower premiums, predictable costs, coordinated care
  • Cons: Limited provider choice, referrals required, no out-of-network coverage

PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)

Offers more flexibility in choosing doctors and specialists. You can see out-of-network providers at a higher cost.

  • Pros: No referrals needed, out-of-network coverage, broad network
  • Cons: Higher premiums, higher out-of-pocket costs for out-of-network

EPO (Exclusive Provider Organization)

A hybrid — no referrals needed, but you must stay in-network (except emergencies).

HDHP (High-Deductible Health Plan)

Lower premiums with a higher deductible. Eligible for a Health Savings Account (HSA), which offers triple tax advantages.

💡 Quick Decision Guide

Choose HMO if: You want low costs and don't mind referrals
Choose PPO if: You want flexibility and see many specialists
Choose HDHP if: You're healthy and want to maximize tax savings with an HSA

Understanding Costs

  • Premium: The monthly amount you pay for coverage
  • Deductible: What you pay before insurance kicks in
  • Copay: A fixed amount for a service (e.g., $30 for a doctor visit)
  • Coinsurance: Your percentage of costs after meeting the deductible (e.g., 20%)
  • Out-of-pocket maximum: The most you'll pay in a year; after this, insurance covers 100%

Health Savings Accounts (HSAs)

If you have an HDHP, an HSA lets you save pre-tax money for medical expenses with triple tax benefits:

  1. Contributions are tax-deductible
  2. Growth is tax-free
  3. Withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free

2026 contribution limits: $4,300 (individual) / $8,550 (family).

Open Enrollment

The annual window to sign up for or change health insurance plans. For ACA Marketplace plans, open enrollment typically runs November 1 – January 15.

Outside open enrollment, you can only enroll with a qualifying life event: marriage, birth of a child, job loss, moving, etc.

Marketplace vs. Employer Coverage

Employer plans are usually cheaper because your employer subsidizes the cost. Marketplace plans offer premium tax credits based on income. Compare both options during open enrollment.
