How to File a Home Insurance Claim (Step-by-Step)

By Lisa Rodriguez March 15, 2026 6 min read

Filing a home insurance claim can feel overwhelming, especially in the aftermath of damage or theft. But following a clear process can make the difference between a smooth payout and a frustrating denial.

Step 1: Document the Damage Immediately

Before you clean up or make any repairs, thoroughly document everything:

  • Take photos and videos from multiple angles
  • Make a detailed list of all damaged or stolen items
  • Save receipts for any emergency repairs or expenses
  • Don't throw anything away until the adjuster has seen it

Step 2: Prevent Further Damage

Your policy requires you to take reasonable steps to prevent additional damage. This might include:

  • Tarping a damaged roof
  • Boarding up broken windows
  • Turning off water to stop a leak
  • Moving undamaged items away from the affected area

Keep all receipts for emergency repairs — your insurer will typically reimburse these costs.

Step 3: Contact Your Insurance Company

Call your insurer's claims line as soon as possible. Most companies have a 24/7 claims hotline. When you call, have ready:

  • Your policy number
  • Date and time of the incident
  • Brief description of what happened
  • Police report number (if applicable)
⏰ Time Matters

Most policies require you to file a claim "promptly" or within a specific timeframe (often 60 days). Filing sooner always leads to better outcomes.

Step 4: Meet with the Claims Adjuster

Your insurer will send an adjuster to inspect the damage. To prepare:

  • Be present during the inspection
  • Walk the adjuster through all damage
  • Share your photos and documentation
  • Provide your home inventory list
  • Ask questions about the process and timeline

Step 5: Get Repair Estimates

Get at least 2–3 independent repair estimates from licensed contractors. Don't rely solely on the insurer's estimate, as it may be lower than the actual cost of repairs.

Step 6: Review the Settlement Offer

After the adjuster's assessment, you'll receive a settlement offer. Review it carefully:

  • Does it cover all documented damage?
  • Is it based on replacement cost or actual cash value?
  • Does it include temporary living expenses if you're displaced?

You can negotiate. If the offer seems low, provide your independent estimates and documentation to support a higher amount.

Step 7: Complete Repairs and Close the Claim

Once you agree on a settlement, you'll receive payment (minus your deductible). Complete all repairs using licensed contractors and keep records of all work done.

📋 Pro Tips

• Keep a home inventory with photos and receipts before you need it
• Review your policy annually so you know what's covered
• Consider hiring a public adjuster for large or complex claims
• Don't accept the first offer if it doesn't feel right
