7 Proven Ways to Lower Your Car Insurance Premium

By Sarah Mitchell March 28, 2026 8 min read

Car insurance is one of those expenses that can quietly drain your wallet if you're not paying attention. The average American spends over $1,700 per year on auto insurance, but with a few smart moves, you can cut that bill significantly.

💡 Key Takeaway

Most drivers are overpaying for car insurance. By shopping around and leveraging discounts, you could save $300–$800 per year without reducing your coverage.

1. Shop Around and Compare Quotes

This is the single most effective way to save money on car insurance. Rates vary dramatically between insurers — sometimes by hundreds of dollars for the exact same coverage. We recommend getting quotes from at least 3–5 different providers every year.

Don't just compare price, though. Look at:

  • Coverage limits and deductibles
  • Customer satisfaction ratings
  • Claims handling reputation
  • Available discounts

2. Bundle Your Policies

If you have home or renters insurance, bundling it with your auto policy can save you 10–25% on both premiums. Most major insurers offer multi-policy discounts, and the savings can be substantial.

"Bundling my auto and home insurance saved me $420 per year. It took 15 minutes on the phone." — James T., WTFLinsurance reader

3. Raise Your Deductible

Your deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before insurance kicks in. Raising your deductible from $500 to $1,000 can reduce your premium by 15–30%. Just make sure you have enough savings to cover the higher deductible if you need to file a claim.

4. Ask About All Available Discounts

Insurance companies offer dozens of discounts that they don't always advertise. Common discounts include:

  • Safe driver discount — clean driving record
  • Good student discount — students with a B average or better
  • Low mileage discount — driving less than 7,500 miles/year
  • Anti-theft device discount — alarm or GPS tracker installed
  • Paperless billing discount — opting for electronic statements
  • Pay-in-full discount — paying your annual premium upfront

5. Improve Your Credit Score

In most states, your credit score directly impacts your insurance premium. Drivers with excellent credit pay an average of 40% less than those with poor credit. Focus on paying bills on time, reducing debt, and checking your credit report for errors.

6. Drive Less with Usage-Based Insurance

If you work from home or don't drive much, usage-based insurance (UBI) programs like Progressive's Snapshot or Allstate's Drivewise can save you up to 30%. These programs track your driving habits and reward safe, low-mileage drivers.

7. Review Your Coverage Annually

Your insurance needs change over time. If your car is older and paid off, you might not need comprehensive and collision coverage. Dropping these coverages on an older vehicle can save you $200–$400 per year.

📋 Action Steps

1. Get 3–5 quotes this week
2. Ask your current insurer about discounts you might be missing
3. Consider raising your deductible if you have emergency savings
4. Review your coverage annually and adjust as needed

The Bottom Line

Lowering your car insurance doesn't mean cutting corners on coverage. It means being a smart consumer — shopping around, leveraging discounts, and making sure your policy fits your current needs. Start with even one of these strategies and you could see meaningful savings within your next billing cycle.
